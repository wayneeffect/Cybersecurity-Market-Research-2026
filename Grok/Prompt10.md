**The global cybersecurity market** (encompassing products, solutions, and services) is currently valued in the **$220–$270 billion range** as of 2025–2026, with strong projected growth driven by rising threats, cloud adoption, AI integration, regulatory demands, and digital transformation.

### Current Market Size (2025–2026)
Different research firms report slightly varying figures due to differences in scope (e.g., inclusion of services, hardware, or specific segments):

- **Fortune Business Insights**: USD **218.98 billion** in 2025 → **248.28 billion** in 2026.
- **MarketsandMarkets**: ~USD **227.59 billion** in 2025.
- **Mordor Intelligence**: ~USD **235.5 billion** in 2025 → **264.43 billion** in 2026.
- **Other estimates** (e.g., The Business Research Company, Cybersecurity Ventures): Around **$250–$306 billion** for 2026 spending.

**Note**: These figures represent the *cybersecurity products and services market*, distinct from the much larger estimated *cost of cybercrime* (projected at ~$10.5–10.8 trillion annually around 2025–2026).

### Projections and Growth
The market is expected to grow at a **CAGR of 9–13.8%** over the coming years, depending on the source:

- **By 2030**:
  - MarketsandMarkets: **USD 351.92 billion** (CAGR 9.1% from 2025).
  - Other forecasts: Up to **$400–500+ billion**.

- **By 2031–2035**:
  - Cybersecurity Ventures: Global spending could exceed **$1 trillion annually by 2031**.
  - Fortune: **USD 699.39 billion** by 2034 (CAGR 13.8%).
  - Mordor/Persistence/etc.: **$470–$560+ billion** range by 2031–2035 (CAGRs ~9–12%).

**Key Growth Drivers**:
- Escalating cyberattacks and ransomware.
- Cloud migration and hybrid work.
- AI-powered threats and AI-driven defenses.
- Regulatory compliance (e.g., GDPR, emerging AI rules).
- Expansion of IoT, OT (operational technology), and critical infrastructure protection.
- North America currently dominates (often ~40–43% market share).

The cybersecurity market remains one of the fastest-growing technology sectors, with robust long-term demand fueled by the expanding digital attack surface. Estimates can vary by methodology, but all major analysts project sustained double-digit or high single-digit growth through the early 2030s. For the most tailored insights, check reports from Fortune Business Insights, MarketsandMarkets, or Cybersecurity Ventures.
