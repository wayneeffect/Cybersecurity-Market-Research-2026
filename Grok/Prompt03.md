**Entry-level cybersecurity jobs** are roles that typically require 0–2 years of direct cybersecurity experience (sometimes considering related IT experience). They serve as starting points for building practical skills in a high-demand field.

True "zero-experience" pure cybersecurity roles are competitive and relatively rare. Many candidates transition from **IT support**, **help desk**, **network administration**, or **systems roles**, or complete certifications, bootcamps, or apprenticeships.

### Common Entry-Level Cybersecurity Job Titles

| Job Title                        | Typical Responsibilities                          | Avg. Salary (US, ~2026) | Key Requirements |
|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|--------------------------|------------------|
| **SOC Analyst (Tier 1 / Junior)** | Monitor alerts, triage incidents, use SIEM tools, follow runbooks | $65k–$95k | Security+, basic networking, shift work often |
| **Cybersecurity Analyst / Specialist** | Vulnerability scanning, basic threat analysis, policy support, monitoring | $70k–$100k+ | Security+, IT background, analytical skills |
| **Cybersecurity Technician / Associate** | Support security tools, endpoint protection, basic admin tasks | $60k–$85k | CompTIA certs, hands-on labs |
| **Junior Compliance / Risk Analyst** | Audits, policy documentation, regulatory checks (e.g., SOC 2, GDPR) | $62k–$90k | Attention to detail, GRC knowledge |
| **Incident & Intrusion Analyst (Junior)** | Initial investigation of breaches, logging | $75k–$100k | Security operations knowledge |
| **Cyber Crime Analyst (entry)** | Basic digital investigations, forensics support | ~$80k–$100k | Analytical mindset |
| **IT Auditor (Junior)** | Review controls, compliance testing | $70k–$95k | Auditing basics |
| **Cloud Security Analyst (Junior)** | Support cloud IAM, configuration reviews (AWS/Azure) | $80k–$110k | Cloud certs (e.g., AWS Security) |

Salaries vary significantly by location (higher in tech hubs or high-cost areas), certifications, and whether the role is government/contractor (often with clearance).

### How to Qualify for These Roles
- **Education**: A bachelor's in IT, computer science, or cybersecurity helps, but it's not always required. Many employers accept equivalent experience or certifications. Bootcamps and apprenticeships (e.g., Per Scholas) are popular pathways.
- **Key Certification**: **CompTIA Security+** is the gold standard for entry-level. Others like CySA+, Google Cybersecurity Certificate, or vendor-specific (AWS, Azure) boost chances.
- **Experience Builders**:
  - IT help desk / support (1–2 years ideal).
  - Home labs, CTFs (Capture The Flag), TryHackMe, HackTheBox.
  - Internships, apprenticeships, or volunteer security work.
  - Personal projects/portfolio (e.g., documenting threat hunts or SIEM setups).

- **Skills in Demand**: Networking fundamentals, Windows/Linux basics, threat detection, basic scripting (Python/PowerShell), cloud concepts, and soft skills like communication and attention to detail.

### Realistic Outlook (2026)
- There are thousands of "entry-level" postings, but many still prefer some IT experience.
- Government, MSPs (managed service providers), and defense contractors often have more accessible entry points, sometimes with paid training.
- Competition is high for remote roles; willingness to work shifts (SOC) or obtain security clearances helps.
- The overall market supports strong growth, but standing out requires demonstrated practical skills beyond just a cert.

**Pro Tip**: Start in general IT if needed, earn Security+ while working, build a home lab, and apply aggressively to SOC Tier 1 or junior analyst roles. Many people break in within 6–18 months with focused effort.

For the latest local data, check **CyberSeek.org** (interactive maps and pathways) or job sites like Indeed, LinkedIn, and ClearanceJobs (for cleared roles). The field rewards persistence and continuous learning.
