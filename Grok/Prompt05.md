**Mid-level cybersecurity jobs** generally require **3–7 years of relevant experience** (often including time in entry-level SOC/analyst or IT roles), along with hands-on skills, certifications beyond the basics, and the ability to work independently on complex tasks.

These roles involve more responsibility than entry-level positions—such as deeper investigations, tool implementation, mentoring juniors, and contributing to strategy—while still falling short of senior/architect or managerial levels.

Salaries vary by location (higher in tech hubs or high COL areas like SF/NY/DC), industry (finance/gov/tech pay more), certifications, and clearances. Total compensation often includes bonuses and equity.

### How to Reach Mid-Level Roles
- **Experience Path**: Start in entry-level SOC Analyst, help desk/IT support, or junior analyst roles. Build 2–4 years of practical experience before moving up.
- **Key Certifications** (mid-level focus):
  - CompTIA CySA+ or PenTest+
  - CISSP (often a goal at this stage)
  - Cloud-specific (AWS Certified Security, Azure Security Engineer)
  - GIAC (GCIH, GSEC), CEH, or OSCP for specialized paths
- **Skills in Demand**: Scripting (Python, PowerShell), cloud platforms, SIEM/EDR tools (Splunk, Sentinel), threat hunting, zero trust, DevSecOps, risk management.

### Outlook
Mid-level professionals are highly sought after because they can deliver immediate value with less hand-holding than juniors. The overall cybersecurity job market remains strong, with thousands of openings in these categories. Specialization (cloud, threat hunting, compliance) often accelerates advancement and pay.

**Advice**: Document your achievements (e.g., “Reduced mean time to detect by X%” or “Led incident response for Y events”). Build a portfolio of projects, contribute to open-source/threat intel, or pursue bug bounties. Network on LinkedIn and consider roles at MSSPs, consultancies, or government contractors for faster growth.

For localized data, visit **CyberSeek.org** (career pathways and salary info by region). Mid-level is where many see significant salary jumps and career momentum in cybersecurity.
