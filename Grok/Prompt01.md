**Cybersecurity** (also called **information security** or **cyber security**) is the practice of protecting computer systems, networks, devices, data, and programs from digital attacks, unauthorized access, damage, or theft.

### Core Goal
The primary objective is to maintain the **CIA triad**:
- **Confidentiality**: Ensuring information is accessible only to authorized users.
- **Integrity**: Protecting data from unauthorized modification or corruption.
- **Availability**: Keeping systems and data accessible when needed.

### What Cybersecurity Protects Against
Cyber threats come from individuals (hackers, insiders), criminal groups, nation-states, and even automated tools. Common threats include:

- **Malware**: Viruses, ransomware, trojans, spyware.
- **Phishing & Social Engineering**: Tricking people into revealing credentials or clicking malicious links.
- **Hacking/Exploits**: Gaining unauthorized access through vulnerabilities.
- **DDoS Attacks**: Overwhelming systems to make them unavailable.
- **Advanced Persistent Threats (APTs)**: Sophisticated, long-term attacks often by state actors.
- **Insider Threats**: Employees or contractors misusing access.
- **Supply Chain Attacks**: Compromising software or hardware vendors to hit downstream targets.

### Key Areas of Cybersecurity

| Area                  | Focus                                      | Examples of Controls                  |
|-----------------------|--------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| **Network Security**  | Protecting networks and traffic            | Firewalls, VPNs, IDS/IPS              |
| **Endpoint Security** | Securing devices (computers, phones, IoT)  | Antivirus, EDR, device management     |
| **Application Security** | Securing software and apps              | Secure coding, WAF, patching          |
| **Cloud Security**    | Protecting cloud environments              | CASB, cloud IAM, configuration management |
| **Data Security**     | Protecting data at rest and in transit     | Encryption, DLP, backups              |
| **Identity & Access Management (IAM)** | Controlling who can access what       | MFA, SSO, zero-trust models           |
| **Incident Response** | Detecting and responding to breaches     | SIEM, forensics, IR plans             |

### Why It Matters
- **Financial impact**: Cybercrime is estimated to cost the world trillions annually.
- **Privacy**: Protects personal data from identity theft and surveillance.
- **National Security**: Critical infrastructure (power grids, hospitals, transportation) is increasingly digital and vulnerable.
- **Business Continuity**: A single breach can destroy reputations and companies (e.g., ransomware locking hospitals out of patient records).

### Fundamental Best Practices
1. Use strong, unique passwords + a password manager.
2. Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) everywhere possible.
3. Keep software and systems updated (patch management).
4. Be cautious with emails and links (verify before clicking).
5. Use antivirus/endpoint protection and firewalls.
6. Backup data regularly (3-2-1 rule: 3 copies, 2 media types, 1 offsite/air-gapped).
7. Follow the principle of **least privilege** and zero trust ("never trust, always verify").

Cybersecurity is both a technical discipline and a mindset. It combines tools, processes, people, and ongoing adaptation because threats evolve constantly (e.g., AI-powered attacks are rising). Professionals in the field often hold certifications like CompTIA Security+, CISSP, or CEH. 

In short: Cybersecurity is the shield for our increasingly digital world.
